4. Rules Of Engagement (ROE)


Rules of Engagement


If we don't nail down written Rules of Engagement in advance, we could run into significant trouble and the penetration test will be seen as less valuable.
• HERE we have an example of how can be formatted such document

• incident handling procedure
We also need a contact(client's employee or manager) with which we can communicate if an incident occurs.
When we conduct a penetration test, incidents(unwanted situation that affect client's environment) can happen especially when we don't have a lot of knowledge about the tested environment. If we plan to do risky test we have to communicate it to the client, so that these can be done in preferred hours where a service stoppage is less painful.
We have always have an incident handling procedure ready:
simplest way is to have the contact of a person on the client's side that is available to coordinate the incident handling.

• Encrypted Communications: agree upon a secured method of communication regarding vulnerability details and the final report. We can use:
▪ encrypted email
▪ security content management technology (e.g: Box)
▪ PGP/GPG, due to the additional technical requirements and the waning usage, it is not very used these days.
▪ NO ZIP files, are less secure because the shared password used to protect the symmetric key in the ZIP file could come under attack. A bad guy could steal the shared password, or it could leak if it is shared among too many people.

• daily debriefing conference call
At the beginning or end of each testing dayor most commonly once or twice a week, schedule a brief session(15-30 minutes long) between the testing team and one or two representatives of the target organization. These calls help to make sure that everyone understands the progress of the test and to identify any issues early on.
During the debriefing, the team should discuss what the team has done so far and what they plan to do next

• Remain in the scope
we make sure that the target of the engagement is property of our client
If the target's client use a shared hosting service(type of web hosting where a single physical server hosts multiple sites), in that case we can't conduct an assessment on that target, unless the hosting provider give us a written permission

• Dates and Time of Day
Agree upon an explicit start date and a finish date and upon acceptable times for testing. if testing are required during off-hours or weekends additional charge incur (up to 25%)

• Automated Shunning functionality by IDS/firewall
I the Rules of Engagement must explicitly take into account any automated shunning functionality the target organization may have. This mean that if IDS or IPS spots an attack, it could reconfigure a firewall to automagically block it.
If Auto shunning is too widely applied could turn inadvertently a penetration test into a denial-of-service attack. Or narrowly focused blocking would just render much of the test useless.
To avoid these conditions, you should do one of the following, and document it in the Rules of Engagement:
◇ Have target organization personnel disengage shunning functionality.
◇ Include an exception for the source addresses of the testing organization.
◇ Announce the test and get an alert when shunning starts so that it can be manually deactivated, allowing traffic from testers through.

• Viewing data on the target
◇ If the penetration tester successfully penetrates a target, should they avoid viewing data on the target?
▪ By default our Rules of Engagement allow the testers to view configuration data from a machine but to avoid looking at any customer and user data on the machine.
▪ With the written permission of the client, in some tests, it may be appropriate to sample small amounts of sensitive user data to confirm access and assess the business impact.
- focus more on counting the number of sensitive records that is possible to access than on retrieving them
◇ If Sniffers are used, explicit that if any personal data are captured from the network will be ignored by the penetration testing team

• What the Rules of Engagement does NOT include
◇ price of the test service
◇ Limitations of Liability: this is better handled in the Contractual Agreement
◇ Intellectual property Ownership: also this is better handled in the Contractual Agreement and explain to who belong the resulting report and the methodology behind it:
▪ target organization
▪ penetration tester
▪ both
◇ Permission to conduct the test: also this is better handled in the Contractual Agreement
