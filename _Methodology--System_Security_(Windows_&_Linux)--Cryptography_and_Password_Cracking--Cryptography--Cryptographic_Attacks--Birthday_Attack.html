Birthday Attack



The birthday attack is an attack used to attempt discover collisions in hashing algorithms(functions), such as MD5 or SHA1.

Explanation of the birthday problem in probability theory


The name is based on the "Birthday Paradox" that states that in a room with 23 people or more, the odds (this can be counterintuitive) are greater than 50% that two will share the same birthday.
We are not trying to match a specific birthday (such as your’s); we are trying to match any birthday.

If you are in a room full of 23 people, you have a 1 in 365 chance of sharing a birthday with each of the 22 other people in the room, for a total of 22/365 chances. If you fail to match, you leave the room and Joe has a 21/365 chance of sharing a birthday with the remaining people. If Joe fails to match, he leaves the room and Morgan has a 20/365 chance, and so on. If you add 22/365 + 21/365 + 20/365 + 19/365 … + 1/365=253, you pass 50% probability.
