The Report



The Report must include:
• Techniques used
• Vulnerabilities found
◇ What do these vulnerabilities mean
◇ Impact and risk for the business
▪ Adjust High/Medium/Low risk indications of vulnerability scanning tools because the risk of a vulnerability have to be addressed in the business context of the target organization
◇ Given a realistic threat that exploits the vulnerability
◇ What risk does it pose
◇ Remediation tips (useful suggestions and technique to remediate to each vulnerability)
▪ How should fixes be prioritized
▪ Configuration changes needed
▪ Architecture alterations needed
• passwords and hashes found
◇ type of the password
◇ common theme in the passwords like the use of the common <Season><Year> or <month><year> scheme
◇ percentage of the passwords cracked
• Exploits used

Report Format


• Executive Summary: This brief up-front matter is meant for executives who may not read the full report, providing them the most important conclusions from the work.
• Introduction: Describes the project at a high level, answering the who, where, when, and why aspects of the project.
• Findings: Presents the actual findings, listed one by one, in the target environment with detailed technical descriptions. The findings are sorted so that the most significant risk issues are discussed first.
• Methodology: It covers the process(what is been done) of the penetration test
• Conclusions and Future Considerations: Summarizes the project results and is reminiscent of the Executive Summary. It also may optionally include future considerations for items to test more comprehensively or defend better.
