Findings



Here the technical results are described. Start with high-risk findings and then move to medium and low risk findings

For each finding:
• list vulnerable system(s) that exhibit the given flaw
◇ IP address
◇ name (if applicable)
• Risk level and how easily the given issue could be exploited:
◇ High (also called Critical)
◇ Medium
◇ Low (also called Informational)
• Explain the business risk (two sentence summary)
• Detailed technical description
◇ what the flaw is
◇ how an attacker could exploit it
◇ impact to the target's business
• Recommendations
For each vulnerability consider: the root cause of the problem, process(es) that allowed the issue to arise, how to prevent from happening again
Provide multiple recommendations: give long-term and short-term fixes, higher and lower cost alternatives and their benefit, higher and lower operational complexity
◇ Applying patches:
vulnerable software usually can be fixed by upgrading to a more recent version or applying a patch.
Give also a command line activity from which is possible to verify that the patch is in place
◇ Hardening systems:
▪ change the configuration of a target machine to harden it from a security perspective
▪ shut off an unneeded service
▪ remove software that doesn't have a defined business need
◇ Applying a filter:
Give also a command line activity from which is possible to verify that the filter is properly filtering
Some vulnerabilities can be mitigated by applying a filter in front of the target service, through a:
▪ network firewall
▪ network-based Intrusion Prevention System
▪ host-based filtering technology:
- personal firewall
- host-based IPS.
◇ Altering architecture:
alter the overall architecture of the target environment:
▪ moving systems around
▪ deploying new kinds of technology
▪ applying filters in a different order
▪ tweaking the flow of data in the environment
• Be careful with including discovered passwords in the report because these reports are often passed around to numerous people in a target organization
◇ Many penetration testers have a policy of describing the characteristics of cracked passwords (such as length or character set) but not including the actual passwords in the report
• Screenshots make the results feel "real" and motivate the organization to improve its security stance
◇ use graphical elements to help focus attention on the most important part
• Network diagrams can be helpful to explain some technical ideas
